How is a Loan EMI Calculated?

EMI stands for Equated Monthly Installment. It is the fixed payment amount a borrower pays back to a financial lender at a specific date every month until the outstanding balance of a loan is cleared. An EMI consists of both a principal portion and an interest portion, representing a gradual amortization of the loan.

The Mathematical EMI Formula

EMI = P × r × (1 + r)n / ((1 + r)n - 1)

Where:

  • P: The Principal loan amount.
  • r: The monthly interest rate (annual interest / 12 / 100).
  • n: The tenure of the loan in total months.
